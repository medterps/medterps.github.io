Be sure to know how to interpret the names of the common viruses and the large variety of respiratory symptoms they cause.

Flu - gripe/influenza
Respiratory sincytial virus - virus sincitial respiratorio
Bronchiolitis - bronquiolitis
Rhynovirus- rinovirus
Adenovirus - adenovirus
Croup - crup
Wheezing - silbido
Stridor - estridor
Dry cough - tos seca
Productive cough - tos productiva (commonly called tos con flemas)
Crackling - sonido crepitante
Shortness of breath - falta de aire
Difficulty breathing - dificultad al respirar

Try to use the same tone as the patient/provider. Tone has a major influence on the meaning of a message and the flow of conversation. A calm statement interpreted with an angry tone by the interpreter can change the course of the encounter for the worst.

Takeaway: if the patient or provider is angry/sad/worried/bored/excited etc. then carry their tone over into your interpretation. That is the message they want to deliver so its the message that we have to interpret; its not up to us to decide what components the message will have.

Insurance is unfortunately an enormous part of healthcare in the U.S. so as interpreters we spend a lot of time talking about it.
Thankfully there are a few key terms you can learn to make these encounters a bit less exhausting.

Deductible - deducible
Premium - mensualidad
Copay - copago
Group number - número de grupo
Policy number- número de póliza
Claim - reclamo
Appeal - apelar v. Apelación n

I say bill as factura by default but sometimes I use bil if the patient seems more comfortable using it.

NEC stands for necrotizing enterocolitis. In Spanish it is enterocolitis necrotizante.

  • necrotizing enterocolitis - enterocolitis necrotizante
  • Multiple sleep latency test - prueba de latencias del sueo
